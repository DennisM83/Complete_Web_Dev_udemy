If you're new to the instant pot world, these are the features of the best model, the DUO60

Features Description
Soup Generally used for soups or if you decided to make your own broth
Meat/Stew a general setting for meat dishes, including(not limited to) a pot roast
Bean/Chili a general setting that cooks chili or beans(good news with this device is you don't have to soak your beans ahead of time)
Poultry a general setting ideal for chicken as it has a lower pre-programmed time. Chicken usually cooks faster than beef.
Rice a setting for cooking rice and parboiled rice.
Multigrain This general setting is used for your wild rice, hard brown rice, mung beans, etc.
Porridge The three bears favorite setting! *crickets chirp* You could just use it for your porridge, of course..
Steam Excellent setting for cooking your veggies or fish!
Slow Cook This is your traditional slow cooker setting, allowing you to cook those classic meals you love.
Pressure This feature allows you to adjust the pressure from High to low according to your recipe.
Adjust This button allows the adjustment of time on your settings. Also, you can adjust pressure keeping time, temp of the slow cooker, as well as, the programs in yogurt
Yogurt On this setting you can make yogurt, pasteurize milk, and make Jiu Niang.
Saute This is one of the buttons you'll be using a lot. This allows for you to cook your diced chicken, onions, beef, or anything else your recipe calls for ahead of time, in the pot, without a pan...no extra washing!
Manual This is the other button you'll be using....A LOT. This allows you to do it your own way! haha! Look, most people have a way the make their recipes and generally it's beyond what these presets can do. Most recipes I found use this setting.
Timer This lets you adjust the time for delayed cooking. This works for all functions EXCEPT "Saute" and "Yogurt".
Keep Warm/Cancel The "Keep warm" function is the state the pot swtiches to when your food as ran through the function time. Cancel allow you to shut it down completely.
